You'll notice several changes which improved the grade of this resume. We place a lot of emphasis on the improving the top third of the resume. Generally, hiring managers will make a snap judgement about the candidate immediately, and they have likely already decided if they will hire you before they finish reading your resume. To maximize your chances, you need to frontload every relevant detail into the opening of your resume.

Here you'll notice we open with a [**professional headline**](https://blog.rezscore.com/crafting-a-professional-headline-67f366c43ba1) of "Entry Level Software Developer", to immediately position this resume towards a target job opening. The beauty of the headline is that it can be easily swapped out depending on the position.

We next list the most recent experience, since hirers want to have a sense of what you are presently doing. We then highlight specific projects and achievements, which we felt were the strongest ways to help Aditya stand out. You'll notice in the original copy Aditya's achievements didn't even make it above the fold, but it is very notable that Aditya regularly achieves awards in programming competitions and built an app with 10,000+ users. View our post on [**responsibilities vs. achievements**](https://blog.rezscore.com/responsibilities-vs-achievements-d5f15f59d470) for further insight.

Finally, note how we take any opportunity to quantify achievements. Tossing specific numbers into your resume has many advantages, not only does it visually enhance boring lines of text, but the specificity provides immediate credibility.

Also of note, the original version did not parse well in some Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS). About of half of all resumes will be reformatted by an ATS before a human sees it, so it is important to make sure the formatting remains pleasant after it is ripped apart.

Altogether, the new resume got results. The resume improved its grade at [RezScore](https://rezscore.com/) and received higher marks in double-blinded experiments.
